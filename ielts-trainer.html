Why Band 7 → 8 is the Hardest Jump

In IELTS, band 8 means you use English at near-native expressive level. The difference between 7 and 8 is qualitative, not quantitative — you don't just need more words, you need to use them flexibly and with precision.

Speaking Part 1 — Introduction Questions

4-5 minutes. Examiner asks personal questions on familiar topics (home, work, hobbies). Aim for 2-3 sentence answers, NOT one-word responses.

Part 1 question banks loading...

Speaking Part 2 — Cue Cards (Long Turn)

1 minute prep + 1-2 minute monologue. The 250 cue cards are organised by category. Use the universal 4-paragraph framework.

Universal Cue Card Framework (4 minutes mental rehearsal):
P1 (~30s): WHO/WHAT — introduce the subject
P2 (~30s): WHEN/WHERE/HOW — give context
P3 (~45s): DETAIL — key features, events, descriptions
P4 (~15s): WHY/IMPACT — why memorable / how you feel
-- / --
Speaking Part 3 — Discussion (Where Band 8 Is Decided)

4-5 minutes of abstract discussion linked to your Part 2 topic. This is the hardest part because it requires extending ideas, hedging, comparing, and predicting.

PEEL Answer Structure for Part 3:
Point — direct answer to the question
Explanation — why this is the case
Example — specific instance (real or hypothetical)
Link — broader implication or counter-perspective
Band 8 Phrases for Part 3
Use: "I'd argue that...", "It's worth considering...", "From a different perspective...", "There's a tendency for...", "While that may be true, one could also say...", "In hypothetical terms..."

Writing Task 1 — Academic (150+ words, 20 min)

Describe a chart, graph, table, diagram, map, or process. NEW for IELTS (PTE doesn't have this exact task). The OVERVIEW paragraph is critical for band 7+.

4-Paragraph Structure:
1. Introduction (paraphrase the question)
2. Overview (2 sentences, MOST IMPORTANT — 2-3 main trends, no numbers)
3. Body 1 (detail group 1 with figures)
4. Body 2 (detail group 2 with figures)
