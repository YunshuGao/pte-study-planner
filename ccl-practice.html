CCL Exam Strategy — Must-Know Tips

These techniques are the difference between 60/90 and 70/90. Read them ALL.

Note-Taking Technique

1Use symbols, not words. Numbers, arrows, abbreviations. You don't have time to write full sentences. Develop your own shorthand: $ = money, -> = go/lead to, X = problem, ✓ = agree.
2Write in the TARGET language. If you hear English, write notes in Chinese characters. If you hear Chinese, write notes in English. This saves you a mental translation step.
3Capture numbers and names FIRST. Dates, amounts, phone numbers, names — these are exact and you WILL lose marks for getting them wrong. Write them immediately.
4Use a split-page layout. Draw a line down the middle of your notepad. Left side = key info. Right side = connecting words and flow.

During the Exam

5Don't panic if you miss a word. Keep going. The examiner scores on OVERALL message transfer. One missed word won't fail you, but freezing for 10 seconds will.
6Paraphrase if you forget the exact term. "Medical imaging procedure" instead of "MRI" is still acceptable. The meaning matters more than the exact word.
7Speak clearly and at a natural pace. Don't rush. Don't mumble. The examiner needs to understand you. Slow and clear beats fast and garbled.
8Use the full time available. If you finish early, add detail you may have missed. Don't just stop — use every second to improve your answer.

Scoring Strategy

963/90 = 70% accuracy. You don't need perfection. You need to transfer the core message of each segment. Focus on the KEY information, not every filler word.
10Minimum 29 per dialogue. Don't bomb one dialogue. Even if you feel one went badly, keep trying hard on every segment. Marks add up.
11Key terms carry more weight. Domain-specific vocabulary (medical terms, legal terms, financial terms) shows the examiner you understand the context. Prioritise learning these.
12Register matters. Use formal language. "Would you be able to..." not "Can you...". "I would like to enquire about..." not "I wanna ask about..."

Common Pitfalls

!Don't add information. Only interpret what was said. Adding your own commentary or explanation loses marks.
!Don't use first person. Say "The caller says they would like to..." or interpret directly. Never say "I think..." unless the speaker said it.
!Don't switch languages mid-sentence. If interpreting to English, stay in English throughout. Code-switching is marked down.
